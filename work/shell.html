Bringing Intelligence to Deals

Insight.ai gives finance leaders the signal they need before they know to ask — surfacing burn-rate risks, budget anomalies, and deal health across 500+ active deals, backed by a unified platform built for a Fortune 10 energy company.

RoleSenior Product Designer
ClientGlobal Energy Major (Fortune 10, Operating in 70+ Countries)
TimelineJan 2025 — Jun 2025

In brief

  • Replaced four parallel deal trackers (spreadsheets, slide packs, legacy tools) with a single workflow-aligned cockpit named the Pacific Platform.
  • Consolidated 500+ active deals into one source of truth across deal management, financial dashboard, resources/time, and budget utilization.
  • Cut review-prep time materially and surfaced deal risk earlier — finance now acts, not just reports.

The reframe

Deal data, time bookings, and budgets lived in four separate trackers. Answering basic questions — which deals are at risk, where are budgets off — took hours of reconciliation. By the time risk surfaced in a steering committee, the window to act had closed.

A dashboard reports the past. A cockpit lets the pilot fly.

That sentence shaped every subsequent decision. Pacific Platform is the cockpit framing, productised.

Information architecture

Four core views, each answering a question finance leaders ask weekly. Same navigation, same vocabulary, same data spine.

Deal Management
Every active deal as a structured record. Portfolio at a glance, drillable to a single deal.
Financial Dashboard
Portfolio KPIs with one-click drill. The "is the spend paying off?" view.
Resources & Time
Who's on which deal, booked time, capacity hot spots.
Budget Utilization
Planned vs. actual spend. Where Insight.AI lives.

Navigation is exception-first. At risk, over budget, capacity constrained are primary chips, not buried filters. From 500+ deals to the few that matter this week — in two taps.

Insight.AI

On the Budget view, Insight.AI surfaces the question finance teams ask weekly — "is this deal off track, and how much warning do I have?" — before they ask it.

  • Burn-rate alerts: "Burn rate up 18% this month. Budget exhaustion projected 3 weeks before closure."
  • Cost-by-category anomalies: flags categories where actuals diverge sharply from plan.
  • Spend-trend forward views: projects FY trajectories from current burn, citing the historical comparison.

Every Insight.AI output has a "why this" affordance — the numbers, the comparison window, the calculation. Trust comes from verification, not faith.

Trade-offs

Single-cockpit cost us scope ambition. Forecasting, scenario analysis, and custom dashboards were all deferred — adoption depended on the cockpit being legible to a finance lead opening it cold. Lean data inputs meant standardising shape across business units; every finance lead lost something small in exchange for visibility nobody had before.

Outcome

Intelligence
AI-backed planning across 1000s of deals — burn-rate risks, anomalies, and deal health surfaced before leaders ask.
One Secure Platform
500+ active deals, four legacy trackers, and Insight.ai — consolidated into a single governed source of truth.
↓ License Costs
Retiring fragmented tools and spreadsheet infrastructure drove measurable cost reduction across the portfolio.
