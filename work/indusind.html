TL;DR

  • Compressed high-friction banking flows (account opening, business loan applications, cross-currency transfers) into few-step, error-proof journeys by collapsing decisions and pre-filling state.
  • Built and rolled out a unified design system underneath — same tokens, same components — across customer mobile, web, and internal staff tools.
  • Component reuse hit ~85% across surfaces. Pending-action drift, the most common bug class on the legacy app, dropped sharply.

The friction audit

The method was uncomfortably simple: pick the 12 most-used flows, walk through each in the legacy app, count every decision a customer had to make. Not every screen — every decision.

  • Apply for Business Loan: 14 decisions across 9 screens. Half were re-asking for KYC the bank already had.
  • Cross-currency transfer: 11 decisions. Three were jargon (correspondent bank, charge bearer) the customer couldn't possibly know.
  • EMI prepayment: 8 decisions — three of them trick questions whose answer changed the next three.

Reframe: stop redesigning screens. Redesign decisions. Every audit asked the same question — "what's the minimum number of decisions to complete this?"

14 → 6

The headline win: Apply for Business Loan, same regulatory shell, same disclosures, fewer decisions.

The seven decisions that disappeared weren't simplified — they were moved off the customer's plate entirely. Replaced with confirmation chips: "Use this PAN" / "Use this address." Customer taps once instead of typing.

"Needs your attention"

A single home-dashboard primitive surfacing exactly what blocks customer progress — pending approvals, incomplete applications, EMIs due. Three rules:

  • Action-led, not status-led. Not "Loan application 50% complete." Instead: "Continue your business loan — 4 fields left."
  • One-tap to resolve. The chip lands the customer on the field that needs them, not on the start of the form.
  • Quiet when there's nothing. No "All caught up!" trophy. The empty state is silence.

Pre-fill + smart defaults

Three patterns across every flow: confirmation chips instead of blank input fields; defaults from history (the beneficiary you transferred to 8 of the last 10 times); live computation as the customer types (loan EMI updates inline, no "Calculate" button).

Killed a wizard mode that walked customers through every complex flow. Tested with users — patronising on simple steps, confusing on complex ones. Replaced with progressive disclosure inside one continuous frame.

System foundation

INDIE shipped on a unified design system — semantic tokens, ~120 components, mobile + web from one library. ~85% component reuse across surfaces. The AI Figma plugins I now use everywhere (Validator, CLAUDE.md Exporter, Generator) were all born here.

The system is the means; flow simplification is the headline. The system itself gets a deeper case study at Torrent Design System.

Trade-offs

Pre-fill works only when the data is right. The first month had a small spike in support tickets when stale KYC surfaced incorrectly — volume dropped sharply once data quality improved. Killing wizard-mode meant some genuinely complex flows ask customers to think a little more in a single step. Accepted.

What I'd change

I'd have written the governance doc on day one, not month three. We had drift across the team's outputs until we sat down and articulated "what counts as a system decision vs. a vertical decision." Cost us ~2 weeks of consolidation. The doc itself is six bullet points; writing it earlier would have been free.

And: I'd have invested in contribution tooling (PR templates, automated changelogs, contribution rituals) sooner. We did them in month four. Done at month one, the contribution velocity would have been double.
